### Telegraph Pole
 
**Artist**<br>
Arthur Dove (American, 1880–1946)
 
**Date**<br>
1929
 
**Medium**<br>
Oil, metallic paint, and pencil on steel plate
 
**Dimensions**<br>
72.4 × 51.5 cm (28 1/2 × 20 1/4 in.)
 
**Category**<br>
Painting
 
**Style**<br>
Modernism
 
**Description**<br>
“Being a wet telegraph pole, some flying leaves and silver.” This is how Arthur Dove briefly described this painting. He evoked the wetness of the scene, making it shimmer and shine by using unconventional materials, like metallic paint and a steel plate. The varied brushwork and pulsating layers of color in the background further enliven the work’s appearance, suggesting that this industrial object has been absorbed into its natural setting.
